Complete Bible Books Reference

Interactive guide to all 66 books of the Bible with summaries

Old Testament (39 Books)

The Pentateuch (Torah/Law)

Genesis

The book of beginnings, covering creation, the fall of humanity, and the early history of God's people through Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, and Joseph.

Exodus

The story of Israel's deliverance from Egyptian slavery under Moses' leadership, including the giving of the Ten Commandments at Mount Sinai.

Leviticus

A detailed guide to worship, sacrificial system, and holy living for the Israelite priests and people, emphasizing ritual purity and moral conduct.

Numbers

Chronicles Israel's 40-year wilderness wandering, including censuses, laws, and the challenges faced before entering the Promised Land.

Deuteronomy

Moses' final speeches to Israel, restating the law and covenant before his death and their entry into Canaan.

Historical Books

Joshua

The conquest and division of the Promised Land under Joshua's leadership after Moses' death.

Judges

A cycle of Israel's spiritual decline, oppression by enemies, and deliverance through various judges over several centuries.

Ruth

A beautiful story of loyalty and redemption following a Moabite woman who becomes part of Israel and an ancestor of King David.

1 Samuel

The transition from judges to monarchy, featuring Samuel the prophet, Saul the first king, and young David's rise.

2 Samuel

The reign of King David, his victories, his sin with Bathsheba, and the consequences that followed.

1 Kings

Solomon's reign and the division of Israel into northern and southern kingdoms, with various kings' stories.

2 Kings

Continued history of the divided kingdom, featuring prophets like Elijah and Elisha, ending with both kingdoms' exile.

1 Chronicles

A retelling of Israel's history from Adam through David's reign, emphasizing David's positive legacy and temple preparations.

2 Chronicles

Focuses on the southern kingdom of Judah from Solomon through the Babylonian exile, highlighting temple worship and reform movements.

Ezra

The return of Jewish exiles from Babylon and the rebuilding of the temple under Ezra's spiritual leadership.

Nehemiah

The rebuilding of Jerusalem's walls and spiritual renewal under Nehemiah's civil leadership.

Esther

A Jewish woman becomes queen of Persia and saves her people from genocide, though God is never explicitly mentioned.

Wisdom Literature

Job

Explores the problem of suffering through the story of a righteous man who loses everything but maintains faith in God.

Psalms

A collection of 150 songs and prayers expressing the full range of human emotion in relationship with God.

Proverbs

Practical wisdom for daily living, emphasizing the fear of the Lord as the beginning of wisdom.

Ecclesiastes

A philosophical reflection on life's meaning, concluding that fearing God and keeping His commandments is humanity's whole duty.

Song of Solomon

A poetic celebration of romantic love between a man and woman, often interpreted as an allegory of God's love for His people.

Major Prophets

Isaiah

Prophecies of judgment and salvation, including many messianic passages about the coming Servant of the Lord.

Jeremiah

The "weeping prophet" warns of Jerusalem's destruction while promising future restoration and a new covenant.

Lamentations

Five poems mourning the destruction of Jerusalem and the temple, traditionally attributed to Jeremiah.

Ezekiel

Visions and prophecies during the Babylonian exile, emphasizing God's holiness, judgment, and eventual restoration of Israel.

Daniel

Stories of faith under foreign rule and apocalyptic visions of future kingdoms and God's eternal kingdom.

Minor Prophets

Hosea

Uses the prophet's marriage to an unfaithful wife as a metaphor for God's relationship with unfaithful Israel.

Joel

Describes a locust plague as a sign of coming judgment and calls for repentance, prophesying the outpouring of God's Spirit.

Amos

A shepherd-prophet condemns social injustice and religious hypocrisy, emphasizing God's demand for justice.

Obadiah

The shortest Old Testament book, prophesying judgment against Edom for their treatment of Israel.

Jonah

A reluctant prophet learns about God's mercy toward repentant enemies, including the great city of Nineveh.

Micah

Denounces injustice while prophesying the Messiah's birth in Bethlehem and God's requirements: justice, mercy, and humility.

Nahum

Prophesies the destruction of Nineveh as judgment for Assyria's cruelty and oppression.

Habakkuk

A prophet questions God about injustice and learns to live by faith despite difficult circumstances.

Zephaniah

Warns of coming judgment but promises restoration for a humble remnant who trust in the Lord.

Haggai

Encourages the returned exiles to prioritize rebuilding the temple over their own comfort and prosperity.

Zechariah

Visions and prophecies encouraging temple rebuilding while looking forward to the coming Messiah and His kingdom.

Malachi

The final Old Testament book, calling for spiritual renewal and promising the coming of Elijah before the "great and dreadful day of the Lord."

New Testament (27 Books)

The Gospels

Matthew

Presents Jesus as the promised Messiah and King, emphasizing His fulfillment of Old Testament prophecies, written primarily for Jewish readers.

Mark

The shortest and likely earliest Gospel, portraying Jesus as the suffering Servant who came to serve and give His life as a ransom.

Luke

A detailed, orderly account emphasizing Jesus' compassion for outcasts, women, and Gentiles, presenting Him as the perfect man and Savior.

John

Focuses on Jesus' divine nature with seven "I Am" statements and signs, written to prove that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God.

Church History

Acts

The history of the early church from Jesus' ascension through Paul's ministry, showing the gospel's spread from Jerusalem to Rome.

Paul's Letters

Romans

Paul's systematic explanation of the gospel, covering sin, salvation by faith, sanctification, and Christian living.

1 Corinthians

Addresses various problems in the Corinthian church, including divisions, immorality, spiritual gifts, and the resurrection.

2 Corinthians

Paul defends his apostolic ministry and encourages the church through his own sufferings and God's comfort.

Galatians

Strongly defends salvation by faith alone against those who would add works of the law to the gospel.

Ephesians

Emphasizes the church's unity and believers' position "in Christ," with practical instructions for Christian living.

Philippians

A joyful letter written from prison, encouraging contentment, unity, and having the mind of Christ.

Colossians

Combats false teaching by emphasizing Christ's supremacy and sufficiency for salvation and spiritual growth.

1 Thessalonians

Encourages a young church in their faith and provides teaching about Christ's second coming.

2 Thessalonians

Corrects misunderstandings about Christ's return and encourages perseverance through persecution.

1 Timothy

Instructions for young pastor Timothy on church leadership, false teaching, and pastoral ministry.

2 Timothy

Paul's final letter, encouraging Timothy to remain faithful and continue preaching the Word despite opposition.

Titus

Guidance for establishing church leadership and maintaining sound doctrine in Crete.

Philemon

A personal letter asking Philemon to forgive and receive back his runaway slave Onesimus as a brother in Christ.

General Letters

Hebrews

Demonstrates Christ's superiority to Old Testament institutions and encourages perseverance in faith despite persecution.

James

Emphasizes practical Christianity, showing that genuine faith produces good works and righteous living.

1 Peter

Encourages Christians suffering persecution to follow Christ's example and live holy lives as strangers in this world.

2 Peter

Warns against false teachers and encourages spiritual growth while affirming the certainty of Christ's return.

1 John

Tests of genuine faith including love for others, obedience to God's commands, and belief in Jesus as God's Son.

2 John

A brief warning against false teachers who deny Christ's incarnation, emphasizing truth and love.

3 John

Commends Gaius for his hospitality and warns against Diotrephes' prideful leadership.

Jude

A strong warning against false teachers who have infiltrated the church, calling believers to contend for the faith.

Prophecy

Revelation

Apocalyptic visions revealing Christ's ultimate victory over evil, the final judgment, and the establishment of God's eternal kingdom.
